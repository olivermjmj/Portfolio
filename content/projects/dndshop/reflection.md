# Final Project Reflection

This project helped me understand backend development much better in practice than I think smaller exercises would have. Because I worked on it over several weeks, I could feel how one decision affected the next layer later on. What started as entities and DAOs gradually turned into a full backend with DTOs, external API integration, services, async methods, and controllers.

The part I am most satisfied with is that the project ended up with a clearer structure than it had in the beginning. Especially the service layer and DTO refactoring made a big difference, because they made the code easier to organize and easier to extend. I also think working with async methods taught me a lot, even though it made the project more difficult and forced me to go back and change parts of the setup.

The biggest lesson for me was probably that testing should come earlier. I could clearly feel that it became harder to add and reason about tests after more of the structure was already built. I also learned that architecture decisions sound simple in theory, but become much harder once they have to work across many classes and layers in a real project.

Overall, I think the project became a solid backend foundation with a proper layered structure. If I had more time, I would focus on stronger endpoint testing, cleaner async error handling, and more refinement of the final API. But as a semester project, I think it gave me a much better understanding of how a backend is actually built and connected from top to bottom.
